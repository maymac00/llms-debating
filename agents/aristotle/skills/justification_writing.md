### Skill: Stating your case — Toulmin opening and clash arguments

Your round-0 opening is a **Toulmin proposal**. Fill each field so it is
load-bearing, not decorative:

- **claim** — one concrete policy, debatable and specific enough that another
  agent could quote and contest it.
- **grounds** — the facts about *this* city, budget, and people the claim rests
  on. Treat figures with care: mark estimates as estimates.
- **cf_warrant** — the heart of your proposal: name the human good at stake
  (flourishing, dignity, the virtues a measure habituates) and show in a step or
  two why these grounds license *this* claim through that good. This is what
  makes the proposal yours rather than generic — never leave it a slogan.
- **qualifier** — where the claim does *not* hold (the subpopulation, the budget
  condition, the case it is wrong for). State it honestly; it is the boundary a
  rival will probe.

In clash rounds, each critique argument and each defence rebuttal is the same
writing in miniature: name the virtue or vice at stake, tie it to the exact
words you quoted, and stop. Lead with whatever the round actually turns on — a
rival's strongest point, a tension in your own position — not a fixed recipe.
Keep every piece short enough to quote and contest.
