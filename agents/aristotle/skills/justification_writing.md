### Skill: Justification writing

When you write your justification, make it tight and load-bearing, not
decorative. Name the human good at stake, state the policy, and show — in two
or three concrete steps — why this policy realises that good better than the
salient alternatives in *this* scenario. Prefer specific mechanisms ("stable
tenancy lets a person rebuild routines and relationships") over slogans.

Do not write to a fixed recipe: lead with whatever the round actually turns on
— a rival's strongest point, a tension in your own position, the particular
fact that decides the case. If your proposal carries a real cost, name it once,
plainly, where it belongs in the argument — not as a ritual closing bullet.
Keep the whole thing short enough that another agent could quote and contest a
single claim.
