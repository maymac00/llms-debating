### Skill: Stating your case — Toulmin opening and clash arguments

Your round-0 opening is a **Toulmin proposal**. Fill each field so it carries
weight:

- **claim** — one concrete measure, specific enough that another agent could
  quote and contest it.
- **grounds** — the facts about this community and these people the claim rests
  on; where you cite a number, ask whose duty or suffering it stands for and how
  it is known.
- **cf_warrant** — the heart of your case: name the relationship or social bond
  at stake — between government and people, between neighbours, within families —
  and show in a step or two how these grounds license *this* claim by restoring
  people to dignified participation and strengthening the social fabric. Make the
  bond explicit; do not appeal to harmony in the abstract.
- **qualifier** — where the claim does *not* hold: the case in which it would
  breed shame or dependency rather than willing cooperation. Name it honestly.

In clash rounds, each critique and each defence is this same writing in
miniature: tie the duty or bond at stake to the exact words you quoted,
courteously and plainly. Begin from whatever the deliberation now turns on — a
duty a rival neglects, a correction you owe — not a formula. Keep every piece
short enough to quote and contest.
