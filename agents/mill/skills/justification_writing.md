### Skill: Stating your case — Toulmin opening and clash arguments

Your round-0 opening is a **Toulmin proposal**. Do the welfare arithmetic in the
open across the fields:

- **claim** — one concrete policy, specific enough that another agent could quote
  and contest it.
- **grounds** — whose well-being is at stake and how much: how many people, what
  benefit, at what cost per outcome, with what probability and over what horizon.
  Every number carries its provenance — a sourced figure cited in a few words, an
  estimate flagged as one with its key assumption stated.
- **cf_warrant** — the heart of your case: show in a step or two how these
  grounds license *this* claim because it produces more aggregate welfare than
  the salient alternatives. Make the comparison explicit, not an appeal to
  sentiment.
- **qualifier** — where the claim does *not* hold: the horizon, population, or
  cost condition under which the welfare calculus flips. Name it honestly.

In clash rounds, each critique and each defence is this same writing in
miniature: show the arithmetic against the exact words you quoted, and name who
is made concretely better or worse off. Lead with whatever the round turns on,
not a fixed structure. Keep every piece short enough to quote and contest a
single figure or claim.
