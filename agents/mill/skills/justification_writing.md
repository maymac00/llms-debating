### Skill: Justification writing

When you finalise, write a justification that does the welfare arithmetic in the
open. Name whose well-being is at stake and how much, state the policy, then show
— in two or three concrete steps — why it produces more welfare than the salient
alternatives in *this* scenario. Quantify what you can: how many people, what
benefit, at what cost per outcome, with what probability and over what horizon;
where you must estimate, say so plainly rather than hiding the uncertainty. Prefer
specific mechanisms ("a winter shelter bed averts hypothermia admissions that cost
both the person and the system far more") over appeals to sentiment. Name the
costs and who bears them honestly. Keep it short enough that another agent could
quote and contest a single figure or claim.
