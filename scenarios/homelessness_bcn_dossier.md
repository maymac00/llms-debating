# Policy Brief: Allocating Barcelona's Marginal Homelessness Windfall

Barcelona, 2026. The December 2025 street count recorded **1,982 people sleeping
rough** in the city — a 98% increase since 2008 — and the wider 2022 census
found 4,197 people in homelessness situations including settlements and
municipal resources. The structural backdrop is national: Spain has the highest
rent-to-income ratio in the European Union and a social housing stock of about
2% against an EU average near 9%.

The city already spends **over €50 million per year** on homelessness under its
2024–2027 strategic framework, which includes the committed continuation of the
Primer la Llar Housing First programme. **That base budget is fixed and is not
under discussion.** What is under discussion: a one-off **€10 million**
settlement from the Generalitat must be allocated for the coming year across
the candidate lines below.

## Candidate lines (documented facts from the city dossier)

1. **Additional Housing First places.** 96%+ housing retention among
   participants. But expansion is hard-constrained by housing stock: the
   programme grew only from 50 to 76 places in nine years, and the dossier
   records case-manager turnover and stock scarcity as persistent limits. Each
   place serves one person from the most damaged, longest-term profiles.
2. **Women-only Housing First (Rosario Endrinal model).** Ten places exist
   since 2024 for women with over a year on the street and combined mental
   illness and addiction — a documented gap in mixed services. Very intensive
   support; very small reach.
3. **Eviction prevention (SIPHO mediation + housing aids).** Over 12,500
   households served since 2015; demand exceeds supply. Dossier caveat: of
   households passing through the city's temporary accommodation, **85% exit
   without a stable housing solution**.
4. **Low-threshold night centres in every district.** Recommended by the city
   ombudsman (2020) and demanded by the December 2025 plenary agreement.
   Reaches people who refuse or cannot access other services, including the
   undocumented. Produces no housing exits by itself.
5. **Asylum-seeker accommodation (Nausica model).** A documented gap in the
   national reception system leaves asylum seekers waiting up to six months
   with no entitlement, forcing many into rough sleeping; Catalonia has 642
   reception places. The city's 92 supplementary places reduce but do not
   eliminate this.
6. **Private stock mobilisation (Hàbitat 3 / rental pool).** Landlord
   incentives up to €6,000 per unit plus renovation subsidies mobilised 150
   units by 2021. Dossier caveat: 86% of real-estate agents are documented as
   accepting or facilitating discrimination against migrant applicants.

**Unit costs per place or per household are not in the dossier. Do not invent
them.** Argue in percentage shares and in terms of who is reached and who is
not.

## Task

Propose an allocation of the €10 million in percentage shares across **at most
three** of the six lines (your proposal), and justify the allocation — and the
exclusions — from the standpoint of your conceptual framework.
