# Council Vote: Confirm or Repeal the Tourist-Apartment Phase-Out

Barcelona, 2026. In 2024 the city decided not to renew any of its roughly
**10,000 tourist-apartment licences**, phasing them all out by 2028–29 so the
flats return to the long-term rental market. The decision is now under legal
and political attack, and the council must vote to **confirm** or **repeal**
it. There is no abstention.

## The case for confirming

- Rents in pressure zones have been driven up 30–40% amid exponential growth
  of short-term rentals, displacing residents — and housing cost is the
  documented upstream driver of homelessness in a country with the EU's
  highest rent-to-income ratio and a 2% social housing stock.
- 12.6% of the city's population is recorded as having had their housing
  right violated; eviction-prevention services report demand exceeding supply.
- Ten thousand flats is of the same order as the city's entire public housing
  stock (11,600 units after seven years of major investment): no construction
  programme can add comparable supply on any comparable timescale.

## The case for repealing

- The dossier records only an **expected** long-term rent moderation; the
  effect of the ban on rents — let alone on rough sleeping — is indirect and
  unproven, while the costs are immediate.
- Thousands of owners, many small, invested lawfully under licences the city
  itself issued; legal challenges are under way and compensation exposure is
  real but unquantified.
- Enforcement against illegal short-term rentals already fails at current
  scale; the ban multiplies the enforcement burden and may simply push the
  market underground.
- Tourism income supports livelihoods across the city, including precarious
  ones; the people who lose are not the people who gain.

No figures beyond those above are documented. Do not invent any.

## Task

State your vote — CONFIRM or REPEAL, with at most one attached condition — as
your proposal, and justify it from the standpoint of your conceptual
framework, including what your framework says to those your vote harms.
