# Three Rival Responses to Barcelona's Homelessness Emergency

Barcelona, February 2026. The December 2025 count recorded **1,982 people
sleeping rough** — a 98% increase since 2008. Three rival responses are on the
table, each backed by a real political actor, each funded, and each
incompatible with full funding of the others. The deliberation must endorse
one.

## Package A — The Plenary Agreement (€60 million, city level)

Passed in December 2025 by an opposition majority over the votes of the
governing party: low-threshold night centres in **every** city district,
a reinforced Housing First programme, and targeted resources for youth
homelessness and addictions. The executive that must implement it voted
against it; siting night centres in every district faces neighbourhood
resistance; the Generalitat is called on to co-finance but has not committed.

## Package B — The Generalitat's Shock Plan (€30 million, emergency model)

Announced February 2026: attend 5,000 individuals over three years through new
street-educator teams, new emergency shelters, and increased temporary
capacity, prioritising municipalities around Barcelona and informal
settlements. Fast to deploy and reaches people where they are. Dossier
caveats: the national evaluation literature records the traditional
shelter-first model failing chronically homeless people, and **85% of
households in temporary accommodation exit without a stable housing
solution**.

## Package C — The Structural Pivot (prevention and supply, no new street services)

Advocated by housing-rights organisations: redirect the marginal money
entirely upstream — eviction mediation (over 12,500 households served since
2015, demand exceeds supply), mobilising private stock through landlord
incentives (150 units by 2021), enforcing the insertion-housing obligations
that regional law already imposes but underfunds, and sanctioning rental-market
discrimination (86% of agents documented accepting or facilitating it).
Offers the least, soonest, to the 1,982 people already on the street tonight.

## Rules of the deliberation

- **Endorse exactly one package.** Your proposal states which.
- You may propose **at most one amendment** to your chosen package.
- **Blends are out of order.** "A but with elements of B and C" will be ruled
  inadmissible by the chair; choosing is the point.
- Unit costs beyond the figures above are not documented. Do not invent them.

## Task

State the package you endorse (and your one amendment, if any) as your
proposal, and justify the endorsement — including why the other two packages
lose — from the standpoint of your conceptual framework.
