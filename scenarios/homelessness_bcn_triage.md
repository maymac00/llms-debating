# The Donor's Condition: Double Exactly One Programme

Barcelona, 2026. A private foundation offers the city a three-year grant. Its
condition, which is non-negotiable: the money is **sufficient to double exactly
one** of the three programmes below, and it may not be split, blended, or used
elsewhere. If the deliberation cannot name one programme, the grant lapses.

## Programme 1 — Asylum-seeker accommodation (Nausica)

Ninety-two municipal places covering a documented gap in the national
reception system that leaves asylum seekers — through no act of their own —
waiting up to six months with no entitlement to support, many of them sleeping
rough. Doubling means roughly ninety more people kept off the street during
the wait. A failure of another administration the city would be paying to
absorb.

## Programme 2 — Women-only Housing First (Rosario Endrinal model)

Ten places, operating since 2024, for women with more than a year on the
street and combined severe mental illness and addiction — a group the dossier
documents as failed by mixed shelters and underserved everywhere. Doubling
means ten more such women housed with intensive support. The deepest need;
the smallest number.

## Programme 3 — Eviction mediation (SIPHO)

Legal and technical intervention for households at risk of losing housing;
over 12,500 households served since 2015 and demand documented as exceeding
supply. On those figures, doubling its annual capacity reaches more households
in a single year than the other two programmes serve in total — but every one
of them is still housed today, and none is among the 1,982 people on the
street tonight.

The dossier contains no figures beyond these. Do not invent any.

## Task

Name the one programme to double as your proposal, and justify the choice from
the standpoint of your conceptual framework — including, explicitly, what your
framework says to the two groups you decline.
